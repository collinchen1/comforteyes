Dietary Tips


  • Iodine is an essential mineral that plays a crucial role in thyroid hormone production. However, excessive iodine intake may exacerbate hyperthyroidism in some individuals.
  • Goitrogens are substances that can interfere with thyroid function by inhibiting iodine uptake. They are found in certain foods, such as cruciferous vegetables (e.g., broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, kale, and Brussels sprouts).
  • Caffeine and Stimulants: Some people with hyperthyroidism may be sensitive to stimulants, including caffeine, as they can exacerbate symptoms like palpitations, nervousness, and restlessness.
